7 Types of Plastic

1. PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate)

Commonly used in beverage bottles and food containers. Recyclable and safe for single use.

2. HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene)

Found in milk jugs and detergent bottles. Durable and widely recyclable.

3. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)

Used in pipes and medical equipment. Difficult to recycle due to chemical additives.

4. LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene)

Common in plastic bags and wraps. Flexible but less recyclable.

5. PP (Polypropylene)

Used in food containers, bottle caps, and straws. Heat resistant and partially recyclable.

6. PS (Polystyrene)

Found in disposable cups and packaging. Lightweight but not easily recyclable.

7. Other (Miscellaneous Plastics)

Includes polycarbonate and bioplastics. Often non-recyclable and used in specialized products.
